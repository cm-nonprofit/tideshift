The Problem

Plastic doesn't stop at the shoreline.

More than a third of the trash washing up on California beaches is single-use plastic — bottle caps, straws, food wrappers, fishing line. Most of it breaks down into microplastics that sea otters, fish, and seabirds mistake for food.

The harder truth: most of it never had to reach the water. It came from communities five, fifteen, or fifty miles inland, traveling down storm drains and creeks. Solving this means building habits — and that starts with the kids who'll inherit this coastline.

Our Story

From a single classroom presentation to a coastal movement.

TideShift began as a high school project documenting plastic on a single Half Moon Bay beach. It grew because every cleanup turned into a classroom invitation, every classroom into a school partnership, and every partnership into another stretch of cleaner shore.

01

The Shoreline Survey

What started as a science-fair project — counting and categorizing plastic items along a 1-mile beach transect — revealed patterns nobody on our block was talking about.

02

The First Cleanup

We invited classmates. Twelve people showed up. We filled forty-three trash bags in two hours and realized this couldn't be a one-time event.

03

The Classroom Pivot

A 4th-grade teacher asked us to present. Then six more teachers did. Education became as central to the mission as the cleanups themselves.

04

The Nonprofit

In 2024 we formalized as TideShift to recruit volunteers, support more schools, and run plastic-reduction campaigns at the source — not just the shore.

What We Do

Four programs working together up and down the coast.

Each program is volunteer-led, school-friendly, and designed to scale by handing the playbook to the next student who wants to start one.

Cleanups

Saturday Shorelines

Monthly volunteer cleanups across Half Moon Bay, Pacifica, Santa Cruz, and Monterey. Every cleanup ends with a sort-and-data session: we weigh, log, and report what we collect to support state policy work.

Education

Sea Lab Workshops

Hands-on K–8 marine ecosystem workshops co-designed with local teachers. Kids dissect kelp, build food webs, and run mini plastic-decomposition experiments that take the lesson home with them.

Advocacy

Source Reduction Campaigns

We partner with cafés, school cafeterias, and city councils to swap single-use plastics for reusable or compostable alternatives — and we track the savings publicly so others can replicate.

Field Trips

Tidepool Discovery Days

Free guided field trips for Title I schools that wouldn't otherwise reach the coast. Students meet anemones, hermit crabs, and sea stars — and leave with a notebook full of their own observations.

Meet the Founder

Built by a student. Run with a community.

Marisabel Testing

Founder · Computer Science Student

Marisabel started TideShift as a high schooler who couldn't stop noticing what the tide brought in. With a Computer Science focus, she's building the data tooling — from cleanup-logging apps to school dashboards — that lets a small volunteer team run programs at a regional scale.

Her goal: make it easy enough for any student in any coastal town to spin up their own chapter, and hard enough for plastic to keep ending up where it doesn't belong.

"You don't need a degree to start. You need a beach, a Saturday, and a willingness to count what you find."
